Q: What is Navkar Mantra?
A: Shree Navkar Mantra is the most auspicious and powerful mantra amongst all the mantras in Jainism.

Q: What are the words of the Navkar Mantra?
A: The Navkar Mantra is:
Namo Arihantanam
Namo Siddhanam
Namo Aayariyanam
Namo Uvajjhayanam
Namo Loe Savva Sahunam
Eso Panch Namukkaro
Savva Pavappanasano
Mangalanam Cha Savvesim
Padhamam Havai Mangalam

Q: What is the significance of Navkar Mantra?
A: Navkar Mantra is the most auspicious and powerful mantra in Jainism. It removes sorrows, gives energy and good luck, makes you happy, peaceful, and wise, and helps you gain the virtues of Dev and Guru.

Q: When should I recite Navkar Mantra?

- 8 Navkar: When you wake up in the morning.
- 7 Navkar: When you go to bed at night.
- 3 Navkar: Before eating food, before leaving home, before going to school, when you get scared, when you are sad, when you see someone in pain, and when you think good of someone.

A: You should recite 7 Navkar when you go to bed at night.

A: You should recite 3 Navkar before eating food.

Q: How many times should I recite Navkar Mantra before going to school?
A: You should recite 3 Navkar before going to school.

Q: When should I recite 3 Navkar?
A: Recite 3 Navkar before eating food, before leaving home, before going to school, when you get scared, when you are sad, when you see someone in pain, and when you think good of someone.

--- CHAPTER 5: WHO IS A JAIN SADHU BHAGWANT? (Pages 11-13) ---
===================================================

Q: Who is a Jain Sadhu Bhagwant?
A: A boy or a man after taking Diksha is called Sadhu Bhagwant. A girl or a woman after taking Diksha is called Sadhvi Bhagwant.

Q: What is a Sadhvi Bhagwant?
A: A girl or a woman after taking Diksha is called Sadhvi Bhagwant.

Q: What is Diksha in Jainism?
A: Diksha is the initiation ceremony in Jainism through which a person renounces worldly life and becomes a Sadhu Bhagwant (monk) or Sadhvi Bhagwant (nun) to follow Bhagwan Mahavir's principles and attain Moksh.

Q: How does a Jain Sadhu Bhagwant look?
A: A Jain Sadhu Bhagwant:
- Wears white clothes.
- Uses Rajoharan (Ogha) to save small insects.
- Uses Muhpatti while talking.

Q: What is Rajoharan or Ogha?
A: Rajoharan (also called Ogha) is an instrument used by Jain Sadhu Bhagwant to gently sweep the ground to save small insects from being stepped on, showing compassion for all living beings.

Q: What is Muhpatti?
A: Muhpatti is a cloth covering worn over the mouth by Jain Sadhu Bhagwant while talking. It is used to avoid harming tiny insects through breath.

Q: Why do Jain Sadhus take Diksha?
A: Jain Sadhus take Diksha for two reasons:
1. To follow Bhagwan Mahavir's principles.
2. To attain Moksh (liberation).

Q: What is Moksh in Jainism?
A: Moksh is the state of complete liberation from the cycle of birth and death in Jainism. Jain Sadhus take Diksha and follow strict principles to attain Moksh.

Q: Where does a Jain Sadhu Bhagwant live?
A: Jain Sadhu Bhagwant lives in the Upashray. They do not have any permanent home.

Q: What is an Upashray?
A: An Upashray is the place where Jain Sadhu Bhagwant and Sadhvi Bhagwant stay. It is like a dharamshala or resting place for monks and nuns.

Q: What does Jain Sadhu Bhagwant eat?
A: Jain Sadhu Bhagwant takes food in small proportions from many houses, just like a cow grazes grass in small proportions from everywhere. This practice is called Gochari (Go = cow). They go to different houses for Gochari and eat whatever they receive.

Q: What is Gochari?
A: Gochari is the practice by which Jain Sadhu Bhagwant takes food in small proportions from many different houses, similar to how a cow (Go) grazes grass from everywhere. The word Gochari comes from "Go" meaning cow.

Q: What is my duty towards Sadhu-Sadhviji Bhagwant?
A: Your duties towards Sadhu-Sadhviji Bhagwant are:
1. Say "Matthaen Vandami" whenever you see Sadhu-Sadhviji Bhagwant.
2. Perform Guruvandan to Sadhu-Sadhviji Bhagwant in Upashray.
3. Request them for Gochari and show them your house.

Q: What should I say when I see a Jain Sadhu or Sadhvi?
A: You should say "Matthaen Vandami" whenever you see Sadhu-Sadhviji Bhagwant.

Q: What is Matthaen Vandami?
A: Matthaen Vandami is the greeting/salutation said to Jain Sadhu-Sadhviji Bhagwant when you see them. It is a respectful way of bowing to them.

Q: What is Guruvandan?
A: Guruvandan is the act of paying respects to Sadhu-Sadhviji Bhagwant at the Upashray. It is an important duty of every Jain child and adult.

Q: Do Jain Sadhus have a permanent home?
A: No, Jain Sadhus do not have any permanent home. They live in Upashray and move from place to place.

Q: What clothes does a Jain Sadhu wear?
A: A Jain Sadhu Bhagwant wears white clothes.

Q: Why do Jain Sadhus use Rajoharan?
A: Jain Sadhus use Rajoharan (Ogha) to save small insects from being harmed, as a practice of non-violence (Ahimsa) towards all living beings.

Q: What is the difference between Sadhu Bhagwant and Sadhvi Bhagwant?
A: Sadhu Bhagwant is a male monk — a boy or man who has taken Diksha. Sadhvi Bhagwant is a female nun — a girl or woman who has taken Diksha.

A: Jeev is the one who has life. Jeev takes birth and dies, has senses (sense of touch, taste, smell, sight and hearing), experiences pleasure and pain, and has knowledge.

A: The characteristics of Jeev are:
1. Jeev takes Birth and Dies.
2. Jeev has Senses (Sense of Touch, Sense of Taste, Sense of Smell, Sense of Sight, and Sense of Hearing).
3. Jeev experiences Pleasure and Pain.
4. Jeev has Knowledge.

Q: What are examples of Jeev?
A: Examples of Jeev are: Humans, Animals, Birds, Ants, Mosquitoes, Trees, Fruits, and Flowers.

Q: Are trees and plants Jeev?
A: Yes, trees, fruits, and flowers are also Jeev because they have life.

Q: What is Ajeev in Jainism?

Q: What is Prabhu Darshan?
A: Prabhu Darshan is the act of visiting and paying respects to Prabhu (the Jain deity/Tirthankar) at the Jin Mandir. It is an important daily spiritual practice for Jains.

Q: When should we do Prabhu Darshan?
A: We must do Prabhu Darshan every morning.

Q: What should we say during Prabhu Darshan?
A: We must bow our head and say "Namo Jinanam" when we do Darshan of Prabhuji.

Q: What is the meaning of Namo Jinanam?
A: Namo Jinanam means "I bow to the Jinas (the victorious ones)" — it is the salutation said while doing Darshan of Prabhuji at the Jin Mandir.

Q: What should we do after Prabhu Darshan?
A: After doing Prabhu Darshan, we must do Dhoop and Deepak Pooja and then give three Khamasamans.

Q: What is Khamasaman?
A: Khamasaman is a form of respectful bowing or prostration done before Prabhuji after completing Prabhu Darshan. We give three Khamasamans.

Q: How many Pradakshinas should one give to Prabhu?
A: One should give three Pradakshinas to Prabhu.

Q: What is Pradakshina?
A: Pradakshina is the act of circumambulating (going around) the Prabhu or deity in the temple as a form of worship and respect. We should give three Pradakshinas to Prabhu.

Q: What should we recite while doing Prabhu Darshan?
A: We must recite Stuti while doing Prabhu Darshan.

Q: What is Stuti?
A: Stuti is a prayer or hymn of praise recited while doing Prabhu Darshan at the Jin Mandir.

Q: What is Dhoop and Deepak Pooja?
A: Dhoop and Deepak Pooja is the offering of incense (Dhoop) and a lamp (Deepak/Divo) to Prabhuji as part of worship after doing Prabhu Darshan.

Q: What should we NOT do during Prabhu Darshan?
A: During Prabhu Darshan, we should NOT:
1. Disobey your parents and act as you like.
2. Play around.
3. Shout — speak softly.
4. Perform Dhoop or Deepak Pooja alone — take help of elders.
5. Spoil anybody's Sathiya.
6. Disturb others.
7. Ring the bell (Ghant) again and again.

Q: Can children do Dhoop or Deepak Pooja alone?
A: No. Children should not perform Dhoop or Deepak Pooja alone. They must take the help of elders.

Q: What is Sathiya in Jain temples?
A: Sathiya is the auspicious Swastik symbol drawn in Jain temples. One should not spoil anybody's Sathiya.

Q: What is the complete sequence of Prabhu Darshan?
A: The complete sequence is:
1. Visit the Jin Mandir every morning.
2. Bow your head and say "Namo Jinanam."
3. Recite Stuti.
4. Do Dhoop and Deepak Pooja (with elder's help).
5. Give three Khamasamans.
6. Give three Pradakshinas to Prabhu.

--- CHAPTER 13: GURU VANDAN VIDHI (Pages 27-28) ---
===================================================

Q: What is Guru Vandan Vidhi?
A: Guru Vandan Vidhi is the correct procedure (Vidhi) for performing Vandan (respectful bowing) to the Guru (Sadhu-Sadhviji Bhagwant).

Q: When should Guru Vandan be done?
A: Guru Vandan should be done only when Guru Bhagwant is sitting on their Aasan (seat).

Q: What are the steps of Guru Vandan Vidhi?
A: The 7 steps of Guru Vandan Vidhi are:
1. Give 2 Khamasaman.
2. Then recite Shree Ichchhakara Sutra.
3. Give one more Khamsaman if Sadhu Bhagwant has been respected by a Padvi of Aacharya, Upadhyay, Gani or Panyas.
4. Recite the Abbhutthio Sutra: "Ichchha-karena Sandisaha Bhagwan! Abbhutthiomi, Abbhintara — Raiyam Khameum? Devsaiam Khameum? Ichchham, Khamemi Raiyum/devasiam."
5. Put the right hand first on the ground and bow your head down and recite Shree Abbhuthio Sutra.
6. Then give one Khamasaman.
7. Ask Guru Bhagwant — Shatame Sahebji.

Q: What is Ichchhakara Sutra?
A: Ichchhakara Sutra (also called Shree Ichchhakara Sutra) is a sacred Jain sutra recited during Guru Vandan Vidhi after giving the first 2 Khamasamans.

Q: What is Abbhutthio Sutra?
A: Abbhutthio Sutra is a sacred Jain sutra recited during Guru Vandan Vidhi. The words are: "Ichchha-karena Sandisaha Bhagwan! Abbhutthiomi, Abbhintara — Raiyam Khameum? Devsaiam Khameum? Ichchham, Khamemi Raiyum/devasiam."

Q: How many Khamasamans are given in Guru Vandan?
A: In Guru Vandan, 2 Khamasamans are given at the start, then one more if the Sadhu Bhagwant holds a Padvi of Aacharya, Upadhyay, Gani or Panyas, then one more after bowing — totaling at least 3 to 4 Khamasamans.

Q: What do you say at the end of Guru Vandan?
A: At the end of Guru Vandan, you ask Guru Bhagwant — "Shatame Sahebji" (asking about their well-being).

Q: What is the correct posture for bowing in Guru Vandan?
A: The correct posture is to put the right hand first on the ground and bow your head down completely as shown, while reciting Shree Abbhuthio Sutra.

Q: What is a Padvi in Jainism?
A: Padvi refers to a rank or title held by Sadhu Bhagwant in the Jain monastic order. The ranks mentioned are Aacharya, Upadhyay, Gani, and Panyas. If the Sadhu holds one of these Padvis, an extra Khamasaman is given.

Q: What is Aasan of Guru Bhagwant?
A: Aasan is the seat on which Guru Bhagwant sits. Guru Vandan should only be performed when Guru Bhagwant is sitting on their Aasan.

A: E stands for Ekendriya — living beings with only one sense (touch), such as plants, trees, earth, water, fire, and air bodies.

Q: What is the story of Aimutta Muni about?
A: Aimutta Muni is a Jain story about a young boy named Atimukta Kumar (Aimutta) from Polaspur, who met Shri Gautam Swami, was inspired to take Diksha at a young age, and attained Kevalgyan at just 9 years of age through repentance and spiritual practice.

Q: Who is Aimutta Muni?
A: Aimutta Muni, whose full name was Atimukta Kumar (Aimutta), was a young boy who took Diksha and became Balmuni Aimutta. He attained Kevalgyan at just 9 years of age by destroying the four destructive karmas through repentance and atonement.

Q: Who were the parents of Aimutta in the story?
A: Aimutta's parents were Vijay Sheth and Srimati Shethani, who lived in a town called Polaspur. The Sheth was very pious and hardworking. They had given good and religious values to Aimutta since early childhood.

Q: How did Aimutta first meet Gautam Swami?
A: One afternoon, Aimutta was playing with friends in the hot sun. He suddenly ran away leaving the game. Friends were surprised. Aimutta had seen the Sadhu Bhagwant coming from the front and recognised him by the vessel (patra) in his hand and the values instilled by his parents. He understood the Sadhu had come for Gochari.

Q: Who is Gautam Swami in the Aimutta Muni story?
A: Gautam Swami was the first Gandhar (chief disciple) of Mahavir Swami and an ocean of humbleness. He was the Guru Maharaj whom 8-year-old Aimutta met and invited for Gochari.

Q: What happened when Aimutta asked to hold Gautam Swami's patra?
A: When Aimutta requested Gautam Swamiji "Shall I hold this patra?" — Gautam Swamiji explained that he cannot give his patra to Aimutta because only one who takes Diksha and becomes a Sadhu can carry that patra. This gave birth to many questions and curiosities in Aimutta's mind.

A: After meeting Gautam Swami, Aimutta started doing Satsang (spiritual gathering) with Guru Bhagwant every day. After hearing about Diksha, religion and sin from Gautam Swami, Aimutta's mind wished to take Diksha. With the consent of his parents, he took Diksha and became Balmuni Aimutta.

Q: What is Satsang?
A: Satsang means spiritual gathering or company — being with holy/religious teachers and listening to their teachings. Aimutta started doing Satsang with Guru Bhagwant every day after meeting Gautam Swami.

Q: What mistake did Aimutta Muni make after taking Diksha?
A: One day during the rainy season, Aimutta Muni saw children making paper boats in puddles. Aimutta Muni realised his mistake and felt very sad.

Q: What is Apkay?
A: Apkay refers to the living beings (Jeev) that exist in water. Jain Sadhus cannot touch raw water because it would cause violence (Himsa) to these water-born beings.

Q: How did Aimutta Muni repent for his mistake?
A: Aimutta Muni went to Samavasaran to repent for his mistake. While reciting Iriyavahiyam Sutra to Lord Mahavir Swami, he started repenting deeply as soon as the word "Panaga-daga" (water beings) appeared. By doing such atonement, he kept reducing his sins.

Q: What is Iriyavahiyam Sutra?

Q: What is Samavasaran?

Q: At what age did Aimutta Muni attain Kevalgyan?

Q: What is the moral of the Aimutta Muni story?

Q: What Jain values does the Aimutta Muni story teach?

Q: What were the 4 destructive karmas destroyed by Aimutta Muni?
A: By deep repentance and atonement, Aimutta Muni destroyed the four destructive karmas (Ghati Karmas) — Gyanavaraniya (knowledge-obscuring), Darshanavaraniya (perception-obscuring), Mohniya (deluding), and Antaray (obstructing) — and thereby attained Kevalgyan.

A: Aimutta was 8 years old when he first met Guru Bhagwant (Gautam Swami) and liked him very much.

A: When people came to know Chandkaushik no longer harmed anyone, they came to see him. Some worshipped him and offered milk and food. Some whose family members had been harmed were still angry and threw wood and stones. Because of the milk and food, ants stung him all over his body. Still he lay calm without getting angry. He died a few days later. Due to his control over himself and his anger, all his bad deeds were destroyed and he attained Devlok (heaven).

Q: What is Devlok?
A: Devlok means the realm of heaven. Chandkaushik attained Devlok (heaven) after death because he controlled his anger, bore all pain calmly, and destroyed his bad karma through self-restraint.

Q: What caused Chandkaushik to be born as a snake?
A: It was because of anger and pride in his previous birth that Chandkaushik had to be born as a snake in this life.

Q: What is the moral of the Chandkaushik story?
A: The moral of the Chandkaushik story is: Due to anger and pride in the previous birth, he had to become the Chandkaushik snake in this birth. But after being calm and tolerating anger with equanimity, he attained Devlok. Fear, anger and ego are violence done not towards others but towards oneself. If we give up anger, we too will attain salvation.

Q: What Jain values does the Chandkaushik story teach?
A: The Chandkaushik story teaches: Ahimsa (non-violence), the power of compassion, the dangers of anger and ego, the importance of self-control, equanimity (samata), forgiveness, and that giving up anger leads to liberation.

Q: What does the Chandkaushik story say about anger?
A: The story says: "Fear, anger and ego are violence done not towards others, but towards oneself." Chandkaushik's anger and pride in a past life caused him to be reborn as a venomous snake. When he gave up anger and became calm, he attained Devlok. So we should give up anger.

Q: Who transformed Chandkaushik?
A: Lord Mahavir Swami transformed Chandkaushik through his compassion, fearlessness, and words of love: "O Chandkaushik! Be calm, be calm! Try to understand what you are doing."

Q: Why did Lord Mahavir not get hurt by Chandkaushik's poison?
A: Lord Mahavir was an enlightened being on the path of Kevalgyan. He had no fear or anger — only compassion. His spiritual power and equanimity protected him. When Chandkaushik stung his thumb, instead of blood, milk came out — a miraculous sign of his purity and compassion.

Q: What does "Bujjha Bujjha Chandakaushik" mean?
A: "Bujjha Bujjha Chandakaushik" means "Understand, understand, O Chandkaushik!" — the words Lord Mahavir said to the snake, calling him to wake up to the truth of his actions.

Q: Where was the Chandkaushik story set?
A: The story was set near Vachala village (Vachala Ganv). Lord Mahavir passed through the forest on his way to Vachala village and encountered Chandkaushik there.
